Hong Kong is a Special Administrative Region controlled by the People's Republic of China

It is a city I would like to visit

I have been to Hong Kong once at a very young age, but don't remember it, and there are plenty of reasons for why I would want to visit it again. Hong Kong is known for its shopping malls, streets, and markets, skyscrapers, dimsum, parks, hiking trails, and much more. It is even home to a Disneyland Park and Ocean Park. Hong Kong is also multicultural. It is a Chinese region that was invaded by Britain in 1839, and then ceded to the British Empire in 1842. Then it became a special administrative region in China in 1997. So, its culture consists of a mixture of western and Chinese culture. The food and views sound amazing, and apparently there are tons of things to do there.

Hong Kong Skyline

Hong Kong Skyline is considered to be the most beautiful skyline in the world. Several notable buildings include: International Commerce Centre, Central Plaza, and Bank of China Tower. There is also a light and sound performance called "A Symphony of Lights" that makes use of several skyscrapers and landmarks on a daily basis if the weather is good. Two good ways to enjoy The Symphony of Lights is to view it from Victoria Peak or the Star Ferry.

Tian Tan Buddha Statue

The Tian Tan Buddha Statue is also known as the Hong Kong Big Buddha. It is 34 meters tall and is located in Ngong Ping Village, Lantau Island. It is admired for its artistic contruction and spiritual representation. Visitors have to walk 268 steps to reach it, but the climb is worth it. The statue is 482 meters above sea level, which means that it can potentially offer an amazing view.

Repulse Bay Beach

Repulse Bay Beach is one of Hong Kong's most popular beaches. It has leafy tropical trees, clean water, and soft white sand. Visitors can swim, eat at restaurants, and visit temples dedicated to sea goddesses. This beach is also close to other tourist attractions, such as Victoria Peak, Ocean Park, and Aberdeen.

Dragon's Back Hike

One of Hong Kong's most famous urban hikes is the Dragon's Back Hike. Visitors take an average time of four hours to travel 8.5km. It's also great for sightseeing along the way. Dragon's Back consists of four stops: Shek O Peninsula Viewing Point, Shek O Peak, Dragon's Back and Pottinger Gap, and Big Wave Bay. Visitors can enjoy a relaxing time at a beautiful beach at the end of the hike. Dragon's Back is known for its views, and most of the hike consists of dirt paths and is easy to access and complete.
